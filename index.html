The Gowanus Canal is a federally-designated Superfund site and is considered one of the most polluted waterways in the U.S.

Decades of industrial use, dating back to the late 19th century, has contributed to the pollution of the Gowanus Canal. From gas plants, paper mills, and tanneries to chemical plants, over 30 companies have been identified as Potentially Responsible Parties (PRPs) for the contamination. In addition to chemicals, untreated sewage from Combined Sewage Overflows (CSOs) can flood into the canal after heavy rains. The 10 feet of toxic sludge at the bottom is colloquially referred to as black mayonnaise.

The following list displays the chemical substances the EPA deems Contaminants of Concern (COCs). COCs are considered an “unacceptable risk to human health or the environment.”

This list is based on the Globally Harmonized System of Classification and Labeling of Chemicals (GHS), an international system for establishing criteria on the classification of environmental, physical, and health hazards. There are two signal words to emphasize the level of hazard severity in GHS: “Danger” and “Warning.” Danger indicates more severe hazards while Warning indicates less severe hazards.

BENZO(B)FLUORANTHENE

Danger

WHAT: Hydrocarbon

WHERE:

  • Gasoline exhaust
  • Tobacco and cigarette smoke
  • Coal tar
  • Soot
  • Amino acids and fatty acid pyrolysis products

HAZARD:

  • May cause cancer
  • Very toxic to aquatic life
  • Very toxic to aquatic life with long lasting effects

POLYCHLORINATED BIPHENYLS (PCBs)

Warning

WHAT: Chlorinated Compound

WHERE:

  • Coolants and lubricants in transformers, capacitors, and other electrical equipment
  • Old fluorescent lighting fixtures
  • Electrical devices containing PCB capacitors
  • Old microscope and hydraulic oils

HAZARD:

  • Causes damage to organs through prolonged or repeated exposure
  • Very toxic to aquatic life with long lasting effects [Acute & Long-term]

MERCURY

Danger

WHAT: Metalloid

WHERE:

  • Occurs naturally
  • Manufacturing
  • Dental and medical equipment
  • Fertilizers

POLYCYCLIC AROMATIC HYDROCARBONS (PAHS)

WHAT: Hydrocarbon

WHERE:

  • Occurs naturally (forest fires and volcanoes)
  • Burning coal, oil, gasoline, trash, tobacco, and wood
  • High-temperature cooking, such as grilling
  • Medicines
  • Pesticides (mothballs)

COPPER

Danger

WHAT: Metal

WHERE:

  • Sheet metal
  • Pennies before 1982
  • Agriculture
  • Water treatment

HAZARD:

LEAD

Danger

WHERE:
